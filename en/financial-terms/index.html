Financial Terms Glossary

Quick, practical definitions for the weekly money system. Use these to keep budgets, expenses, savings, and debt decisions clear.

Budget
A pre-plan for income that assigns every dollar to essentials, obligations, savings, and flexible spend before the month starts.
Fixed expenses
Recurring costs with predictable amounts (rent, insurance, internet). Fund these first.
Variable expenses
Necessary but usage-based costs (groceries, transport). Set weekly caps, not monthly guesses.
Flexible expenses
Wants and lifestyle choices (dining, shopping). Primary lever for cutting overspend.
Cash flow
Money moving in and out during a period. Positive cash flow means income exceeds spending.
Net income
What’s left after all expenses and obligations. The most important health indicator.
Emergency fund
3–6 months of essentials saved to handle surprises without debt.
Installment
A scheduled recurring payment on a debt.
Interest
The cost of borrowing money. Prioritize higher-rate debts to reduce total cost.
Purchasing power
What your money can actually buy after inflation. Rising prices lower purchasing power.
Savings
Income you set aside for future needs or goals. Automate transfers within 24 hours of payday.
Investment
Putting money into assets that can grow over time, usually with higher risk than savings.
Diversification
Spreading money across different assets to reduce risk concentration.
Debt management
Organizing balances, fixing payments above minimums, and reviewing weekly to stay on track.
Cash buffer
A small weekly reserve (5–10% of flexible spend) to absorb surprises without raiding savings.
Weekly review
A 10-minute check of top spend categories, one correction for next week, and confirmation that savings/debt stay funded.
