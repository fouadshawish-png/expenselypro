How to Control Impulse Spending (and Still Enjoy Life)

By Fouad Shawish

Open reports (web) Open reports (in-app)

Impulse buys are rarely about the product; they are about state—fatigue, boredom, or stress. The fix is to change the state before the purchase.

Step 1: Set a 24‑hour rule

Any unplanned item above your personal threshold (e.g., $30) must wait 24 hours. Add it to a “later” list inside the app.

Step 2: Pre‑commit limits

  • Weekly fun cap (cash envelope or dedicated card).
  • One “green‑light” category you never exceed (e.g., books at $40/week).

Step 3: Change the cue

  • Uninstall one shopping app; use web only.
  • Turn off push promos.
  • Keep cards out of reach; default to cash for wants.

Step 4: Use micro‑delays

When you’re already at checkout, delay 10 minutes: walk, drink water, recheck your “later” list. Most impulses fade.

Step 5: Post‑purchase audit

Once a week, tag any regretful buys. If you hit 3 regrets in a week, drop next week’s fun cap by 10% and retry.

Scripts that help

  • With friends: “I’m on a 24‑hour rule—if I still want it tomorrow, I’ll grab it.”
  • Online: Move the item to a wish list; schedule a review on Friday.

If you slip

Log it, label it “impulse,” and pair it with a small corrective action (e.g., $20 to savings). The goal is feedback, not shame.
