Weekly Budget Review: 20 Minutes That Save the Month

By Fouad Shawish

A weekly review catches overspending early, keeps you aligned with goals, and prevents end‑of‑month surprises. Here’s a simple, repeatable script.

Setup (one time)

  • Pick a fixed slot (e.g., Sunday 5pm).
  • Use a single dashboard: weekly spend vs. plan, category over/under, cash vs. card, and goal progress.
  • Keep last week’s notes visible.

Step 1: Reconcile (5 minutes)

  • Mark any uncategorized transactions.
  • Match cash receipts; update balances.
  • Check debt and savings transfers posted as planned.

Step 2: Spot signals (7 minutes)

  • Any category >110% of its weekly cap?
  • Did “fun/impulse” exceed its envelope?
  • Are you ahead/behind on debt or savings target?

Step 3: Make one change (5 minutes)

Pick one lever for the coming week:

  • Lower one category by 10%.
  • Shift a non‑urgent purchase to next week.
  • Add a micro‑rule (e.g., no delivery on weekdays).

Step 4: Reconfirm goals (3 minutes)

State the target for the week: “Add $150 to emergency fund” or “Pay extra $100 to card X.” Clarity reduces drift.

Templates

  • Note format: “Groceries +$40 (hosted friends) → offset from dining out next week.”
  • Decision format: “Move gym shoes to March; free $70 this week.”

If you’re behind

  • Cut two wants for 7 days; keep basics untouched.
  • Delay one subscription renewal.
  • Sell or return one recent non‑essential purchase.
