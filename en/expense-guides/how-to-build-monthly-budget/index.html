Build a Monthly Budget You’ll Stick To

By Fouad Shawish · 12 February 2026

A good budget is one you can follow week after week. Start with reality, not generic percentages.

Step 1: Find your real net income

Plan with the lowest recent month, not the highest. Use a safe number you can rely on.

Step 2: Lock essentials first

  • Housing
  • Utilities
  • Core transport
  • Mandatory installments

Step 3: Create flexible buckets

  • Food
  • Fun
  • Unplanned buys
  • Self‑development

Practical split

Example on 9000:

  • Fixed obligations: 3800
  • Variable essentials: 2200
  • Debt payoff: 1300
  • Savings & emergency: 900
  • Discretionary: 800

Step 4: Make it weekly

Convert caps to weekly amounts so they guide daily choices (e.g., food 1200 → 300/week).

Use the Weekly Budget Review System to stay on track.

Step 5: Link to goals and debt

  • Growth goal: savings/emergency
  • Commitment goal: specific debt

Adjust without breaking it

10% rule: move at most 10% between categories when you adjust.

One decision per week: change one line only; momentum beats perfection.

If the budget isn’t working

  • Check daily tracking: Daily expense tracking
  • Handle impulse spikes: Impulse spending control
  • Reshuffle debt priority: Debt control strategy
